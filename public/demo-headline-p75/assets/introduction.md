# Task Instructions

In this study, you will make a series of investment decisions. On each trial, compare Option A with Option B and choose the option you would prefer to invest in. You can maximize your bonus by using the information provided in the headline and the chart to choose the option that you think will perform better over a 60-day time horizon.

Imagine that you have $1,000 to invest on each trial. The investments shown in the task are hypothetical, but after your choice on each trial, we will generate an outcome and show how much you would have made on the investment. Your choices and these generated outcomes will determine your bonus, with no possibility of losing money.

## What the chart shows

The vertical axis shows investment return (%). A return of 0% means the investment ends with the same value it started with. A return of +10% means a $1,000 investment would gain $100. A return of -10% means a $1,000 investment would lose $100.

The horizontal axis shows time. Day 0 is today:

- The left side of the chart shows recent performance before today.
- The right side of the chart shows possible outcomes over the next 60 days.

![Example investment chart](demo-headline-p75/assets/plots/practice-trial-1.png)

## Option A: stock index

Option A is the stock index shown in blue.

A stock index tracks the combined performance of many stocks, so it is often used as a simple measure of how a larger part of the stock market is moving.

On the chart:
- The solid blue line before Day 0 shows Option A's recent return history.
- The blue lines and shaded area after Day 0 show possible future returns for Option A.
- Wider blue projection bands mean the future outcome is more uncertain.

In the main decision trials, each trial also includes a short market headline. The headline gives market context for Option A. It does not describe Option B's fixed payoff rule.

## Option B: structured note

Option B is the structured note shown in green.

A structured note is a financial product whose payoff depends on whether a market condition is met. In this study, Option B depends on whether a market benchmark closes above its current level.

For Option B, on every trial:
- If the benchmark closes above its current level, the investment earns a +10% return on $1,000.
- If the benchmark does not close above its current level, the $1,000 principal is returned, equal to a 0% return.
- The estimated probability that the +10% payout condition is met is shown on the trial. In this study, it will be 75%.

On the chart, Option B is shown by the green horizontal lines:
- The 0% green line is the return if the note does not pay the +10% return.
- The +10% green line is the return if the note pays the +10% return.
- The dashed green line is the average return implied by the stated payout probability: 7.5% for a 75% chance of receiving +10%.

The dashed green line is not a guaranteed return. It is the expected value of Option B's payoff.

## How we determine your bonus

After you choose an option, we generate a hypothetical outcome for that trial based on the information shown in the headline and the chart. For Option B, the payout condition is met with the estimated probability shown on that trial. For Option A, payout condition corresponds to the information shown in the headline and the chart with unknown probability.

We will choose three of these trials at random as the basis for your bonus. On these trials, each hypothetical dollar gained in excess of $50 adds 10 cents to your bonus. There are no negative bonuses. This means your bonus will range from $0 to $15 depending on performance.

In addition to your bonus, you will receive a flat reward of $5 for full participation.
