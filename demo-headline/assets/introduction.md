# Task Instructions

In this study, you will make a series of investment decisions. On each trial, compare Option A with Option B and choose the option you would prefer. You can maximize your bonus by choosing the option that you think will perform better over a 60-day time horizon.

Imagine that you have $1,000 to invest on each trial. The investments shown in the task are hypothetical, but your choices and the outcomes generated by the study will determine your bonus, with no possibility of losing money.

## What the chart shows

The vertical axis shows investment return (%). A return of 0% means the investment ends with the same value it started with. A return of +10% means a $1,000 investment would gain $100.

The horizontal axis shows time. Day 0 is today:

- The left side of the chart shows recent performance before today.
- The right side of the chart shows possible outcomes over the next 60 days.

![Example investment chart](demo-headline/assets/plots/practice-trial-1.png)

## Option A: stock index

Option A is the stock index shown in blue.

A stock index tracks the combined performance of many stocks, so it is often used as a simple measure of how a larger part of the stock market is moving.

On the chart:
- The solid blue line before Day 0 shows Option A's recent return history.
- The blue lines and shaded area after Day 0 show possible future returns for Option A.
- Wider blue projection bands mean the future outcome is more uncertain.

In the main decision trials, each trial also includes a short market headline. The headline gives market context for Option A. It does not describe Option B's fixed payoff rule.

## Option B: structured note

Option B is the structured note shown in green.

A structured note is a financial product whose payoff depends on whether a market condition is met. In this study, Option B depends on whether a market benchmark closes above its current level.

For Option B, on every trial:
- If the benchmark closes above its current level, the investment earns a +10% return on $1,000.
- If the benchmark does not close above its current level, the $1,000 principal is returned, equal to a 0% return.
- The estimated probability that the +10% payout condition is met is 55%.

On the chart, Option B is shown by the green horizontal lines:
- The 0% green line is the return if the note does not pay the +10% return.
- The +10% green line is the return if the note pays the +10% return.
- The dashed 5.5% green line is the average return implied by a 55% chance of receiving +10%.

The 5.5% line is not a guaranteed return. It is the expected value of Option B's payoff.

## How we determine your bonus

After you choose an option, the study generates an outcome for that trial using the payoff information shown to you. For Option B, the payout condition is met with an estimated probability of 55% on every trial. For Option A, the chart and headline provide information about the possible 60-day outcome.

Your bonus is based on your choices and the outcomes generated by the study. Once you make a choice on a trial, that choice is final.
